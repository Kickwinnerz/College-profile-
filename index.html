Mr. Kaura Khan

Sardar Kaura Khan Jatoi (died 1898) was a philanthropic tribal chief and landowner from Muzaffargarh, Pakistan, who used his vast wealth to benefit the public by building roads, wells, and supporting education and the poor. He is known for donating a significant portion of his land in a 1894 will to establish a trust for public welfare, particularly education and health. This land, managed by the Sardar Kaura Khan Trust and subject to state rules, continues to provide benefits to the districts of Muzaffargarh and Rajanpur, though there have been legal issues surrounding the management of the land. Founder & Owner — Mr. Kaura Khan established this college with a vision of providing quality education and modern facilities to the youth of Jatoi and surrounding areas. Under his leadership the college emphasizes strong academics, practical labs, discipline and character-building activities that prepare students for higher education and professional careers.

About Kaura Khan Graduation College

Kaura Khan Graduation College for Boys (Jatoi) is committed to academic excellence, ethical values, and practical skill development. We offer intermediate and undergraduate programs in Sciences, Commerce and Arts. The college provides modern computer and science laboratories, a well-stocked library, experienced faculty, and merit-based scholarships to deserving students. Our mission is to create disciplined, well-rounded graduates who are ready for university and professional life. Kaura Khan Graduation College, also known as Govt. Kaura Khan Associate College, is a government-affiliated educational institution in Jatoi, Muzaffargarh, Pakistan, offering various intermediate and associate degree programs to both male and female students. The college was established by Sardar Kaura Khan to provide educational opportunities in the region and offers programs like F.Sc. (Pre-Medical, Pre-Engineering, ICS) and Associate Degree Programs in Arts and Science. Key Details
